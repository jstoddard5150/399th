399th Army Band Command Team:

CW02 Richard F. Chapman

Cheif Warrant Officer Two Richard Chapman took command of the 399th Army Band, Fort Leonard Wood, MO in September 2013. He holds a Master of Music in Band Conducting from the University of Iowa and a Bachelor of Music in Trombone Performance from the Ohio State University. While at the University of Iowa, he served as graduate assistant within the University Band Department. His responsibilities included assisting with 250-member Hawkeye Marching Band, Iowa Pep Bands, and three Concert Bands. During his undergraduate study at Ohio State, he was a member of many ensembles including the Concert Band, Jazz Ensemble, and Marching Band.

Throughout his collegiate career, he has participated in several noteworthy performances, such as the 2002 Fiesta Bowl National Championship and the 55th Presidential Inaugural Parade with the Ohio State University Marching Band, the 2009 Orange Bowl with the Hawkeye Marching Band, and the 2011 Iowa Bandmasters Association Featured Performance. He has also been a part of five recording projects, most recently the 2012 release "Martyrs of the Faith," with the University of Iowa Symphony Band.

Prior to studying at Iowa, Mr. Chapman was a Sergeant and trombonist in the US Army Training and Doctrine Command (TRADOC) Band and the 8th US Army Band. He served as guest conductor with both the TRADOC and 8th Army Bands several times, significantly, at the Seoul Summer Blast Concert and the 75th Anniversary of the TRADOC Band Music Under the Stars. He also wrote marching drill for the combined 8th Army and 2nd Infantry Division Bands, which was performed at the 2007 Wonju Tattoo, Jump Guro Festival and the 2007 Republic of Korea Army Festival. In 2009, he was a part of a multi-national ceremonial fanfare trumpet ensemble, combining the His Majesty the King's Guard Fanfare Trumpets (Norway) with the TRADOC Fanfare Trumpets.

Richard's memberships include the Phi Kappa Lambda National Music Honor Society, Phi Mu Alpha Sinfonia, the Adjutant General Corps Regimental Association and the National Band Association. Among his military honors are the Meritorious Service Medal, the Army Commendation Medal - one oak leaf cluster, and the University of Iowa Army ROTC Superior Senior Cadet Citation. He is also a 2011 Distinguished Military Graduate and the Honor Graduate of Adjutant General Basic Officer Leadership Course class 012-11.

1SG James Freeman

First Sergeant James Freeman attended the Crane School of Music at the State University of New York, where he majored in music education. 1SG Freeman enlisted in the Army as a French Horn player in September 1992 and attended Basic Training at Fort Sill, OK and Advanced Individual Training at the Armed Forces School of Music at the Little Creek Naval Amphibious Base in Norfolk, Virginia.

p>He holds a Master’s of Science in International Management from the University of Phoenix and a Bachelor’s of Science in Management from the University of Maryland. He is a graduate of the Primary Leadership Development Course, the Basic Non-Commissioned Officer Course and the Advanced Non-Commissioned Officer Course.

His previous assignments include the First Cavalry Division Band, Fort Hood, TX., the United States Army Japan Band, Camp Zama, Japan, the 98th Army Band, Fort Rucker, AL, the First Infantry Division Band, Fort Riley, KS., the Army Ground Forces Band, Fort Bragg, NC., and his current assignment at the 399th Army band in Fort Leonard Wood.

First Sergeant Freeman’s awards and decorations include the Bronze Star Medal, the Purple Heart Medal, the Meritorious Service Medal with 2 oak leaf clusters, the Army Commendation Medal with oak leaf cluster, the Army Achievement Medal with silver oak leaf cluster, the Good Conduct Medal with 2 silver knots, Global War on Terrorism Service Medal, Iraqi Campaign Medal with bronze star, National Defense Service Medal with bronze star, Military Outstanding Volunteer Service Medal , Non-Commissioned Officer Professional Development ribbon with numeral “3” and the Overseas service ribbon with numeral ”3”.
